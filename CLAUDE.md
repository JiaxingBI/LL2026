## Design Context

### Users
Group leaders and team coordinators use this scheduling UI in daily factory operations. Their core job is to arrange and adjust a 4-work / 4-rest shift pattern quickly, with minimal friction and clear confidence about what changed.

### Brand Personality
Intuitive, quick-reacting, handy.

The interface should feel operational and efficient rather than ceremonial. Users should be able to make day-level decisions fast without navigating through unnecessary procedures.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light-mode operational workspace with clean hierarchy, compact controls, and fast-scanning layouts. Interactions should feel responsive and obvious. Avoid anything that feels overloaded, overly decorative, or process-heavy.

Anti-reference: interfaces that require too many steps to perform one action.

### Design Principles
1. Optimize for fast daily edits over formal workflows.
2. Keep high-frequency actions visible and low-friction.
3. Preserve strong table legibility and scan speed in dense scheduling views.
4. Favor keyboard-first interaction patterns where practical.
5. Make state changes obvious and reversible.
